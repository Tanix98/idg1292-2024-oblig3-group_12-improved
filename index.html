Every day around 8 million pieces of plastic makes their way into our oceans.

12 million metric tons of plastic ends up in the ocean each year. That’s the equivalent of more than 100,000 blue whales. By 2050, ocean plastic will outweigh all of the ocean’s fish.

Ocean plastic can be broken into smaller pieces — known as microplastics that go into our food chain. When it eventually degrades the process releases chemicals that further contaminate the sea.

1 in 3 fish caught for human consumption now contains plastic.

Humans constantly inhale and ingest microplastic through contaminated seafood, including fish and shellfish. Additionally, microplastics have been found in tap water, bottled water, and even commonly consumed beverages, such as beer and salt.

What can you do?

  • Reduce Your Use of Single-Use Plastics
  • Buy less plastic and bring a reusable bag
  • Recycling helps keep plastics out of the ocean and reduces the amount of “new” plastic in circulation
